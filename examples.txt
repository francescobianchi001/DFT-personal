Here are some usage examples:

  # Basic 2D orbital plots
  python3 allplotter.py 26                          # Iron, all orbitals
  python3 allplotter.py 10 --orbital 2p             # Neon, only 2p orbital

  # 3D isosurface plots
  python3 allplotter.py 26 --3d                     # Iron, all orbitals in 3D
  python3 allplotter.py 22 --3d --orbital 3d        # Titanium, just the 3d

  # Energy queries
  python3 allplotter.py 6 --energies                # Carbon, full energy breakdown
  python3 allplotter.py 18 --energy total            # Argon, just E_total
  python3 allplotter.py 26 --energy eigenvalues      # Iron, all orbital energies

  # Save/load workflow (useful for heavy atoms)
  python3 allplotter.py 26 --save Fe_data            # Run once, save results
  python3 allplotter.py 26 --load Fe_data.npz        # Instant replot, no DFT
  python3 allplotter.py 26 --load Fe_data.npz --3d   # 3D from saved data
  python3 allplotter.py 26 --load Fe_data.npz --orbital 3d  # Just 3d from saved

  # Compare atoms (run in sequence, save all)
  for Z in 3 6 10 18 26; do python3 allplotter.py $Z --save Z${Z}_data --energies; done

  # Higher resolution grid
  python3 allplotter.py 10 --grid 5000              # Finer grid for accuracy

  Typical workflows:
  - Quick look: just python3 allplotter.py <Z> to see all orbitals
  - Publication figure: run with --save, then --load --orbital 3d --3d to iterate on specific orbitals without re-running DFT
  - Energy comparison: loop over atoms with --energy total to build a table
  - Teaching: use --orbital to show students one orbital at a time, --energies to discuss energy contributions
